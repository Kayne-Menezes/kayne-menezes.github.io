Reflecting on Foundations

27 August 2024

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Both the emphasis on processing our emotions and our ability to regulate our minds. I was surprised how advantageous incorporating this aspect into my journey has been.

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

Three new things:

  • More Open Minded - I've become more open minded about different routes to achieving an outcome.
  • More Organised - I've needed to become more organised to try and avoid the irregularities life throws our way. This organisation as also led me to become less complacent when strategizing a plan forward.
  • Less Complacent - Facing limitations consistently and pushing yourself outside of your comfort zone teaches you humility real fast.

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

The role of values, empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming is often underestimated. These aspects allow us to identify our current weaknesses, bridge those gaps in a productive manner. Processing our emotions is critical to keeping calm under pressure and regulating our thoughts. The greater advantage of those aspects is extending it out to others. For example, I'm a resilient person and being aware of my nature allows me to step up in a higher pressure environment to help my team out.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I can see both perspectives of this question. Through my degree, the work I do and through the experiences I have had, I can see that I've got a reasonably strong understanding of myself and many of these concepts. In saying that, I've definitely learned more about how to relate to people, process my own emotions. I've become a more competent individual through it all. However, I wish I learned these skills earlier as it would have fast tracked alot of my experiences in the right direction. I don't think its a waste of time - especially for many students coming into this course from school. I wish I went through these resources earlier.

Why do you think we, a programming school are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development bootcamp course?

A misconception is often that programming is an anti-social activity. In reality, at many organisations, programming requires a significant amount of coordination and communication to understand a teams vision and help make that a reality. Human skills help us understand ourselves better and how to work with others to create a more positive and productive environment.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

To initiate this process of understanding yourself, you need to confront your weaknesses and inadequacies. That was challenging. To open yourself up and explain to others where there may be alot of room for growth. When I first started I didn't necessarily see it as "room for growth" rather I saw it as showing others where I'm 'weak' or 'incapable'. Only by confronting ourselves and putting this process into practice, I see how I allowed myself to grow.
