Identity & Values

Core Skills

4 August 2024

1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

One time there was an undertow of animosity between two groups of friends due to events which would have caused resentfulness. I decided to step in, be honest and straightforward, address the problem early. The approach was risky and may have escalated things further, but I think the maturity was appreciated and we ended up having a productive conversation on how we move forward together. Although not everyone agreed with each others perspectives, as a whole, we all moved forward from it positively – that is the most important part. I think sometimes this is just a reflection of life, not everything goes the way you expect it, and sometimes a significant part of life is how you react to the events that unfold upon you. Often this takes some courage and character to face these challenges and looking back, I’m glad I had that character to bring everyone together and be the catalyst for progress.

2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My family and my close childhood friends have cultivated the qualities and values that have built my character today. I’ve got a growth-mindset because they are patient and understand that true development takes real time and effort. I’ve become resilient and persevere through adversity in part due to the adversity you face through the clash of culture, inherent differences and the volatile changes occurring through life. My family and friends have been through those with me and we support each other through everything.

3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

As discussed above, I’ve got a growth mindset that keeps me focused on development, I don’t get easily discouraged because I don’t limit myself unnecessarily. Additionally, I’m a resilient person that finds a way to get through difficult situations, I’ll need this trait through this journey as working while studying isn’t easy. I’ve got a lot of self-discipline, I’m risk taking enough to get out of my comfort zone and try new things and I’m independent. Those three traits will help me work diligently to accomplish the goals set before me.

4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

My limitations are sometimes arrogance and complacency. Taking short cuts because I assume I know something or underestimating the time and effort required to complete my task because I overestimate my abilities. There are times where I’m put under extra time pressure due to misunderstanding something and needing to go back through it again to ensure I understand it properly.

5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I’m a lawyer in the infrastructure division of a leading organisation in the construction industry. My role itself is inherently tense and there is often tension either internally to get a desired outcome or externally to balance expectations. There are many tools to negotiate beneficial settlements, work through complex problems to keep a project afloat and motivate teams through distressing times. However, a tool that generally always keeps me winning in these environments is the ability to assess multiple viable approaches from different angles. This is often circumstantial and involves thinking outside the box at times. So far, it has been extremely effective. I think this course is more collaborative, and the most effective strategies are asking for help, helping others and building each other up especially through the more difficult stages of the process.
