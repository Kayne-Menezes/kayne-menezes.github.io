Core Part 4 |Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindsets

18 August 2024

Neuroplasticity:

The principle of neuroplasticity show that our neural connections are strengthened when we repetitively do the same actions and consistently perform.

Engaging with the same topics, consistently working on similar problems, and reflecting on these solutions (what went wrong, what went right) can help solidify our understanding and build up our problem-solving skills.

Exposing your mind to complex problems so your constantly thinking and problem solving can help build these neural connections. Practising all the time is key. Furthermore, its helpful to formulate connections between the things you learn and how it relates to what you already know. This helps to see the usefulness of the knowledge you’ve just gained and how it fits into the bigger picture.

Growth Mindset:

A growth mindset is the view that our intellectual ability is not fixed. You can continue to learn and grow.

In this exercise, I did gain more of an appreciation for the idea that as a society we’re so focused on positive self-talk like “You are gifted!”, “You’re so talented!”, “You’re a natural!”. I never really thought about how those statements can tend to create such a limiting mindset.

I am already integrating the growth mindset into my learning journey. When I’m under immense pressure and I feel like I’m not good enough, I know that if not now, I just need to keep working and I’ll find myself there. There are numerous ways to scale a mountain.
