Core Part 3 | Te Whare Tapa Wha

18 August 2024

The Te Whare model encapsulates foundational principles that are important for our development and growth. The are described and explained below.

  • Taha Tinana – Physical Wellbeing;
  • Taha Hinengaro – Mental Wellbeing;
  • Taha Wairua – Spiritual Wellbeing;
  • Taha Whenua – Family Wellbeing.

Taha Tinana – Physical Wellbeing:

Taha Tinana is your physical wellbeing, its how you take care of your physical body. In my opinion, physical wellbeing also encompasses your ability to push your body to the extent of its capabilities and past those to continue developing.

Keeping my body healthy is immensely important to me. I have a desk job where I sit down most of the day. This can result in the same muscle groups contracting and remaining tensed up. Additionally, your metabolism also slows down. Fat begins to store and can lead to a myriad of health issues.

Every morning, I wake up early before work and go to the gym. An hour of weights and a quick 10-15min run is a daily step toward reaching my fitness goals. Stretching also help keep my muscles relaxed and helps with blood circulation around my body.

Taha Hinengaro – Mental Wellbeing:

Taha Hinengaro is your mental wellbeing. Taha Hinengaro to me, is about how your mind processes its emotions, how you connect your reality with the conceptual possibilities of chaos, instability and our own insecurities, our failure to accurately perceive the world around us.

Discipline is key here. Structuring your lifestyle is incredibly important in providing that sense of stability despite the chaos that can sometimes surround us. Additionally, taking time to process your thoughts, formulating plans for your life (short term and long term) and after all of that, refocusing yourself on the present by meditating. I do this quite often, it helps reassure me in a logical way that I’m moving in the direction I want to go, it helps your envision your purpose and the goals that will get you there.

To keep my mentality strong, I stay consistent with my physical activity, I take cold showers every day (as strange as this sounds – for meditative purposes), I go on walks to process my thoughts.

Taha Wairua – Spiritual Wellbeing:

Spiritual wellbeing can be expressed through beliefs, values, traditions and practices that support self-awareness and identity. Taha wairua provides a sense of meaning and purpose as well as experiencing a sense of connectedness to self, whānau, community, nature and the sacred.

Taha Wairua, to me, is who you are, its your character, the parts that make up your mentality and are like the feet that allow you to run towards your goals. This principle is built from everything, the information you consume, the people you associate yourself with, your own intrinsic motivations and personality. I build upon this by learning about the world around me, I meditate, and connect with people to grow as a person.

Taha Whanau – Family Wellbeing:

Taha Whanau relates to your support network, your family and extended friend groups, your community. I connect with my close group of friends daily, they are my support network and we help each other all the time. My family is always supportive and they’re always there for me when I need it the most.

Whenua – Connection to the Land:

Connection to the land and to nature has been shown to improve mental and physical wellbeing. To me its important to go outside, spend some time breathing in nature, taking walks, running at parks and enjoying the world around us. Getting a little sunlight never hurts!
